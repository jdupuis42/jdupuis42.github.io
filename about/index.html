About

Thanks for stopping by. I’m a musicologist and (mostly amateur) musician currently living in the Boston area.

I graduated from the University of Notre Dame in 2011 and earned my PhD in musicology from Brandeis University in 2021 with a dissertation on composers' importations of popular theater genres into—to paraphrase historian, William Weber—high-prestige music making. My research focuses on music, history and aesthetics in German-speaking lands and the United States during the long(est) 19th century. I’m especially interested in ways that cultural hierarchies interact in, and across, various repertoires. Among other topics, I have researched and written on:

  • late 18th-century popular theater and its influence on chamber and concert music during the 19th century
  • mutual influences between spoken theater and music
  • ways music contributed to aesthetics around revolution in the first half of the 19th century in Europe
  • how music streaming service users form virtual communities (link)
  • how individuals form and express artistic identities, focused on Sophie Schröder
  • structures of commendation and honor for 19th-century German musicians, focused on Clara Schumann
  • gender representation in US-ian classical music radio programming (an ongoing collaborative project with my wife, Kathryn, which will soon be publicly hosted on this website)
  • middlebrow culture, American opera, and Cold War-era middle-class anxieties

Links to representative work in these areas will be posted on this site soon.

Right now, I work as a visiting lecturer of music at Framingham State University, having taught most frequently a course for education majors that gives them tools for integrating music into their (future) teaching. I have also taught at Brandeis University, Harvard University, Longy School of Music, and lectured in Brandeis’s Osher Lifelong Learning program. I also sit on the board of Calliope, an orchestral and choral ensemble in Boston. You can find more information in my CV, found on this site.

I will update this site regularly with various things, including the radio station project, editions of scores I created for performances as a conductor, blog posts, and other things that need a place to live.
