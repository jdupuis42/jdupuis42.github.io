Aaron Copland's Lincoln Portrait in Cincinnati

28 July 2023

I recently wrote a program note for the Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra’s performances of Aaron Copland’s 1942 piece for orchestra and narration, Lincoln Portrait, and in tracing through performances, I’ve been fascinated by the array of people who performed the declamatory narration. Here is a (surely non-exhaustive) list of people who have performed the role just in this city:

Date Narrator Orchestra and Conductor
14 May 1942 William Adams Cincinnati Symphony Orchestra (CSO) and Andre Kostelanetz
13-14 February 1948 Peter Grant CSO and Thor Johnson
21 February 1953 Walter Weidenbacher CSO and Thor Johnson
22 January 1962 George Palmer CSO and Haig Yanghjian (CSO Young People’s Concert)
26-27 February 1965 William Warfield CSO and Max Rudolph
August 1967 Unnamed youth “Youth-Fest” at Nippert Stadium
5 June 1970 Cincinnati City Councilman Myron Bush CSO and Erich Kunzel
4-5 July 1971 Mike Reid, Cincinnati Bengals DL’man CSO Pops and Kunzel
13-14 February 1976 US Senator Robert Taft, Jr. CSO and Francois Huybrechts
14 March 1976 Steve Douglas Cincinnati Youth Symphony and Robert Cowden
14 March 1976 Bob Braun CSO Pops and Kunzel
30 May 1983 Cincinnati Mayor Thomas Brush CSO Pops and Kunzel
4 July 1985 Ohio state Senator Stanley Ronoff (R-Cincinnati) CSO Pops with Kunzel
6-7 September 1985 Pat McInally, Bengals punter CSO Pops with Kunzel
4 July 1987 Ron Reagan (son of President Reagan) CSO Pops with Kunzel
1987 recording Katherine Hepburn CSO Pops with Kunzel
4 July 1990 Charles Kuralt CSO Pops with Kunzel
4 July 1992 Bryant Gumbel (50th anniversary of piece premiere) CSO Pops with Kunzel
7 September 1996 no further details
4 July 1999 Ohio Senate President Richard Finan CSO Pops with John Morris Russell
16-17 June 2000 Cincinnati Mayor Charlie Luken CSO Pops with Kunzel
18 June 2009 Neil Armstrong (had previously performed elsewhere in 1984/88) CSO Pops with Kunzel
9-10 November 2013 Maya Angelou CSO and Louis Langrée (Langrée’s inaugural concert)
2017 tour various CSO

Other performances have taken place in Cincinnati and its vicinity, as well, including one with Nick Clooney, father of actor George Clooney, with the Blue Ash Symphony Orchestra, conducted by Paul Stanbery (who conducted it multiple times).

(Some dates are approximations based on newspaper announcements that lack performance dates. “CSO” signifies either the regular-season orchestra or the Pops. Various sources, primarily: archives of Cincinnati papers hosted at newspapers.com)
